About Me

I am a researcher in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at George Mason University, working under the supervision of Dr. Ferah Munshi. My research focuses on cosmological simulations and dark matter in dwarf galaxies.

Current Research Projects

3D Shape Analysis of Dwarf Galaxies

Investigating the relationship between stellar and dark matter distributions in dwarf galaxy simulations. Our research demonstrates that the 3D shape of stellar distributions closely traces the underlying dark matter structure, particularly in galaxies lacking a stellar disk.

Comparative Analysis of Dark Matter Models

Examining the differences in 3D shapes of dark matter and stellar distributions under two distinct dark matter models:

  • Cold Dark Matter (CDM)
  • Self-Interacting Dark Matter (SIDM) with velocity-dependent cross sections
